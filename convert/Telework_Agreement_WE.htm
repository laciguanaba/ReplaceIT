General conditions of the telework arrangement

The following policy requirements must be met in this telework arrangement:

The employee and the manager have read and agreed to respect the conditions of the Directive on Telework and the CRTC Guidance to Employees - telework in a hybrid workplace,

Participation of both the employee and the employer in this telework arrangement is voluntary and may be cancelled or suspended at any time by either party with reasonable notice, or amendments can be made subject to management approval and agreement of both parties.

This telework agreement will be reviewed at least annually and will be considered when determining whether the telework agreement will be extended or terminated.

The manager and employee understand that the terms and conditions of employment, provisions of the relevant collective agreement, and application of existing policies and legislation will continue to apply in telework situations.

The manager and employee will maintain regular communications and establish an annual performance agreement.

The employee has been provided direction on how to establish a safe and ergonomic work environment, and any questions and concerns raised by the employee have been answered and dealt with to the satisfaction of both parties.

Specific conditions of the telework arrangement

Information Technology and Cybersecurity

The organization will provide the service or instruction necessary for the installation, upgrading and maintenance of its hardware and software.

The organization will provide remote IT help desk services, however depending on the support required, the employee may have to go to the worksite for service.

The employee has accepted to abide by the CRTC IT Acceptable Use Policy and the guidance found in the CRTC mandatory cybersecurity training, Discovering Cyber Security (S035).

The equipment, data and files remain the property of the organization and will be returned to the organization upon request of the employer.

  Employee and manager have discussed the above IT telework and cybersecurity conditions prior to the approval of the telework location.

Information Management

The employee agrees to save all information of business value (that is, files of any kind that capture decision-making or reporting on operations, performance or accountability) in DM, the CRTC's official information management (IM) repository.

  Employee and manager have discussed the above IM telework conditions prior to the approval of the telework location.

Security & Privacy

The employee and the manager have discussed the safe custody and control of sensitive information. The employee agrees to comply with the employers security policies, standards and procedures and to take all reasonable care to protect sensitive government information and assets against unauthorized disclosure, loss, theft, fire, destruction, damage or modification.

The employee will also:

·        protect and manage records and other sensitive information stored on devices and transmitted across external networks

·        keep government property and information safe, secure and separate from their personal property and information while they are working at their telework location

·        inform their manager immediately in the event that any information or Crown assets are lost, stolen or damaged, or if a suspected privacy breach has occurred

·        manage any personal information held by the institution in accordance with requirements established under the Privacy Act and related policies, directives and guidelines

·        obtain prior approval from their manager before working at any telework location (domestic or foreign) not included in this agreement

  Employee and manager have discussed the above security and privacy telework conditions prior to the approval of the telework location.

Occupational Health and Safety Checklist

By checking the boxes below, the employee understands, confirms and accepts their responsibilities and the conditions specified in this section:

Home office environment

  

The desk, chair and other accessories are suitable to the needs of the employee.

The computer furniture, shelves, cabinets and bookcases are sturdy and properly installed (for example, anchored to the wall, if necessary).

  

The workstation is adjusted properly: the desk, chair, computer monitor, and keyboard are at appropriate heights (for example, the employees head and wrists are in neutral positions).

The employee is aware of how to prevent musculoskeletal injuries (for example, the employee takes breaks to avoid extended hours of repeated motions or of being in the same body position).

  

The lighting is properly arranged and appropriate for the work of the employee (for example, there is no reflection or glare from the computer monitor).

The employee has assessed levels of ventilation, temperature, light and sound, and determined they are adequate for supporting a productive work environment.

The employee has ensured that there are no tripping hazards, such as frayed or wrinkled carpets, obstructed halls, walkways or entries, and that any guardrails are properly installed.

Electrical safety

  

Any extension cords are in good condition and positioned properly.

  

There are no cords or cables that could act as tripping hazards.

  

Outlets are grounded and not overloaded.

  

Outlets are safe and are not in need of repair.

  

There is surge protection for electrical equipment.

  

There is sufficient ventilation for electrical equipment.

Fire protection

  

There is a smoke detector that is checked regularly near the employees workstation at the telework location.

  

Paper materials and any chemicals are stored safely away from all heat sources.

  

Any hazardous product is properly stored to prevent accidental exposure.

  

The employee has complied with prescribed fire safety standards and emergency measures.

  

An evacuation plan has been established.

Other

  

First aid supplies are adequate.

  

A regular method of communication (daily) is established between the employee and manager to ensure that the employee is safe and healthy.

  

The emergency contact has been identified and recorded, and the office contact knows how to reach them in the event of an emergency.

  

The employee is aware to immediately report to the manager any accident or injury that occurs during working hours or while the employee is performing a work activity assigned by the employer.
